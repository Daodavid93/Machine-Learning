1) Tailor Series of function with one variable¶

Consider the function $f(x)$ difined by a power series in form : $$ f(x) = \sum_i^{\infty}c_n(x-a)^n$$ $f(x)$ as expansion $$f(x)= c_0 + c_1.(x-a)^1+ c_2.(x-a)^2+ c_3.(x-a)^3+ c_4.(x-a)^4...$$ we observed that $f(a)=c_0$ Moreover
$\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;$ $f^{1}(a)=c_1 $
$\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;$ $f^{2}(a)=2.c_1$
$\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;$ $f^{3}(a)=3.2.c_1$
$\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;$ $f^{4}(a)=4.3.2.c_4$
$\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;$ $f^{5}(a)=5.4.3.2.c_1$
in general : $$ f^{n}(a)=n!.c_n$$ or $$c_n = \frac{f^{n}(a)}{n!} $$
then we achive for taylor function
$$T(x) = f(a) + \frac{f^{1}(a).(x-a)^1}{1}+\frac{f^{2}(a).(x-a)^2}{2.1}+\frac{f^{1}(a).(x-a)^3}{3.2.1}...\frac{f^{n}(a).(x-a)^n}{n!}$$

That is called ** Tailor series** if $a=0$ the expanion $ f(x) = \sum_i^{\infty}c_n(x-0)^n= \sum_i^{\infty}c_n(x)^n$ is called ** Macloren series :

$$f(x)= f^(0)+ \frac{f^{1}(0)}{1}.x^3+\frac{f^{2}(0)}{2.1}.x^2+ \frac{f^{3}(0)}{3.2.1}.x^3...\frac{f^{n}(0)}{n!}.x^n$$

$$f(x) =\sum_i^{\infty}\frac{f^{n}(a)}{n!}.x^n $$

Let's to see Tayilor expansion of $f(x) = x^2$
when $a=1$ $$f(a)=1 , f'(a) = 2 , f''(a)=2,f'''(a)=0$$

$$f(x) = 1 + 2(x-1) +\frac{2(x-1)^2}{1.2}$$

Taylor approximation of function with 2 variables¶

Let f be an infinitely differentiable function in some open neighborhood around (x, y) = (a, b)
$$f(x_1,x_2)\approx f(a,b)(x-a) + f_{x_1}(a,b)(x-a)+ f_{x_2}(a,b)(x-b) + \frac{1}{2!}[f_{x_1x_1}(a,b)(x-a)^2 +2f_{x_1x_2}(a,b)(x-a)(x-b)+f_{a,b}+f_{x_2x_2}(a,b)(x-b)^2]...$$ or in more compact form : $$ f(x) = f(a) + [(x-a)\nabla f(a)] +[(x-a)H(x)(x-a)]...$$ where $a = <a,b> $ $x=<x_1,x_2>$ and $$ Hesian \; H = \begin{bmatrix} f_{x_1x_1}&f_{x_1x_2} \\ f_{x_2x_2} & f_{x_2x_1} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial^2{f}}{\partial{x_1}\partial{x_1}} & \frac{\partial^2{f}}{\partial{x_1}\partial{x_2}} \\ \frac{\partial^2{f}}{\partial{x_2}\partial{x_2}} & \frac{\partial^2{f}}{\partial{x_2}\partial{x_1}} \end{bmatrix}$$
